Carbonara Recipe

Description:

is a pasta dish made with fatty cured pork, hard cheese, eggs, salt, and black pepper. t is typical of the Lazio region of Italy. The dish took its modern form and name in the middle of the 20th century.

Ingredients:

  • Spaghetti (or any long pasta)
  • Eggs (usually 2 whole + 1 yolk per serving)
  • Pancetta or guanciale (Italian cured pork — can substitute with bacon if needed)
  • Pecorino Romano cheese (grated)
  • Parmesan cheese (optional, for milder flavor)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt (for pasta water only — the cheese and pancetta are already salty)

Steps:

  1. Cook the pasta: Boil spaghetti in salted water until al dente. Reserve about one cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. Cook the pancetta: In a large pan, cook pancetta or guanciale over medium heat until crisp. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. Make the sauce: In a bowl, whisk together eggs, grated Pecorino Romano, and plenty of black pepper until smooth.
  4. Combine everything: Add hot drained pasta to the pan with pancetta (off the heat). Quickly pour in the egg-cheese mixture and toss vigorously, adding a splash of pasta water to create a creamy sauce.
  5. Adjust consistency: Add more cheese or a bit more pasta water until silky and smooth — not runny.
  6. Serve immediately: Top with extra Pecorino and freshly cracked black pepper.
